# Page Recipes

These page recipes define reliable starting structures for common storefront pages. Use them as blueprint defaults and source guidance, not as rigid layouts.

## Home

Purpose: introduce the brand, direct shoppers into the catalog, and establish trust.

Recommended structure:

1. Hero with a clear value proposition.
2. Featured collection or featured product entry point.
3. Supporting brand story or key benefits.
4. Social proof, editorial content, or educational content.
5. Newsletter or final call to action.

Keep the mobile version short and scannable.

## Product

Purpose: answer purchase questions and convert interest into action.

Recommended structure:

1. Product media and primary purchase controls.
2. Price, variant selection, and shipping or fulfillment cues.
3. Benefit details and supporting copy.
4. Collapsible specs, materials, or FAQs.
5. Related products or complementary items.

Make sure keyboard focus and variant selection remain usable on smaller screens.

## Collection

Purpose: help shoppers compare options and narrow their choices.

Recommended structure:

1. Collection heading and summary.
2. Filter and sort controls.
3. Product grid with fast scan behavior.
4. Optional merchandising content or featured subcategory blocks.
5. Pagination or infinite browse pattern, depending on catalog size.

The collection page should feel useful even when the catalog is small.

## Recipe Rule

If the page recipe depends on a blueprint choice, put that choice in the blueprint so the generated theme stays reproducible.
